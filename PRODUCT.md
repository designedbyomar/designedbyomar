# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, founders, and design leaders evaluating Omar Tavarez for Head of Design, Principal Product Designer, Senior/Staff, or fractional design-engineering roles. They arrive with high judgment, limited time, and a low tolerance for generic portfolios. They are looking for signal: strategic thinking, production-quality craft, and evidence that he can lead from ambiguity to shipped product.

Secondary: potential collaborators, peers, and recruiters who may forward the site.

## Product Purpose

Public portfolio and hiring artifact for Omar Tavarez. The site exists to convert qualified visitors into conversations. Success means a hiring manager finishes a case study and reaches out — not because the site was impressive, but because the work was undeniable and the presentation earned their trust.

The public repo and open codebase are part of the artifact: they demonstrate AI-assisted design-engineering workflow and production-grade thinking, not just visual output.

## Brand Personality

Senior, editorial, precise. Calm confidence. The work speaks quietly and clearly. No performance, no hype, no spectacle — just evidence.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Dribbble portfolio** — pastel mockups, gradient text, soft-shadow floating devices, rounded-everything aesthetic. Looks like a student exercise, not a principal-level portfolio.
- **Over-engineered creative developer site** — WebGL, scroll-jacking, noise textures, heavy JS showcasing technique over communication. Craft as distraction.
- **SaaS marketing site** — feature-grid sections, CTA-everywhere layout, "10x your workflow" copy energy. Treats the portfolio like a product marketing page.

## Design Principles

- **Clarity earns trust before novelty does.** Every design decision should make the work easier to read and evaluate, not harder. Hierarchy, legibility, and focus over visual invention.
- **Practice what you preach.** The site is the proof of craft — accessible, performant, mobile-tight, semantically correct. A portfolio that's inaccessible or slow undermines the case studies about senior UX.
- **Show the work, not the process of showing it.** Navigation, layout, and motion should be invisible. The reader should be thinking about the case study, not the site.
- **Restraint is a decision, not a default.** The calm, dark, editorial look is chosen. Don't drift toward it by inertia; maintain it with intention.
- **Human voice over portfolio language.** Copy should sound like Omar, not a LinkedIn summary or an AI template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum across all routes. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for all animation, canvas, and scroll behavior. Keyboard navigability for all interactive elements including drawers, accordions, and focus restore. Sufficient color contrast in both dark and light themes.
